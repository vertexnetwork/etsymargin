## Handmade jewelry has the most volatile margin on Etsy

Jewelry is one of Etsy's most-listed categories and one of its most under-priced. The reason: material costs swing wildly (sterling silver moves with spot price, gold-fill moves with manufacturing), but sellers anchor their retail prices to whatever they listed at six months ago. Meanwhile, Etsy's fees are structurally fixed and growing as a share of revenue when sellers discount.

## A $38 sterling stud earring sale, fee by fee

Take a typical handmade stud earring: $38 sale price, $4 shipping charged, $9 in materials, $3.25 in actual postage. Gross: $42. Etsy's cut:

- Listing fee: $0.20
- Transaction fee (6.5% of $42): $2.73
- Payment processing (3% + $0.25): $1.51
- Off-Site Ads at 15%: $6.30

That's **$10.74** before you cover materials and shipping. After all costs: **net profit ~$18.26, margin 48% on item price.** Healthy — but only if you're not running discounts.

The danger zone for jewelry is sales and coupon codes. A 25% discount on this earring drops the sale price to $28.50, gross to $32.50, and your Etsy fees barely move (they're percentage-based on the lower number, so they shrink modestly). After fees and materials: net profit collapses to about $11. You just gave up 40% of your profit for a 25% discount.

## The common mistake: pricing for "perceived handmade value"

Etsy buyers will pay more for genuinely handmade pieces, but only if the listing makes the craft visible — process photos, video of soldering or wire-wrapping, materials sourced and named. Sellers who price low to "compete" lose twice: lower revenue per order _and_ lower perceived value, which makes future price increases harder.

The other common failure: not factoring metal price changes into pricing. Sterling silver doubled from 2020 to 2024. Sellers who didn't reprice ate that entire cost increase out of margin.

## How to fix it

- **Reprice quarterly** based on current spot prices for precious metals.
- **Avoid sitewide coupons.** Bundle discounts (buy 2 get 10% off) preserve average order value better than blanket sales.
- **Charge shipping at cost.** Inflating it loses the buyer; absorbing it costs you the 6.5% transaction fee on the higher item price.
- **Off-Site Ads are optional below $10k revenue.** Many small jewelry shops find that opting out raises net revenue, even if total orders dip — the math usually works out.
