## Handmade earrings: light shipping, heavy material variance

Earring economics on Etsy are deceptive. Shipping is cheap (poly mailer, $3–4 USPS first-class). Materials are wildly variable: brass and copper run $0.50–2 per pair; sterling silver and 14k gold-fill run $4–12 per pair. Design time per piece is fast (10–20 minutes for a competent maker) but the prep work (sourcing, anti-tarnish coating, photography) is what actually eats the day. The fee math behaves predictably; the margin reality depends entirely on which material tier you're operating in.

## A $22 handmade earring sale, fully decomposed

$22 pair + $3.50 shipping = $25.50 gross. Materials $6 (mid-range — silver-plated brass with semi-precious stones), real shipping $3. Total cost ~$9. Etsy fees on the gross:

- Listing fee: $0.20
- Transaction fee (6.5% of $25.50): $1.66
- Payment processing (3% + $0.25): $1.02
- Off-Site Ads at 15%: $3.83

Etsy total: **$6.71 — 26.3% of gross.** After cost of goods and shipping: net profit is **$25.50 − $9 − $6.71 = $9.79 — about 38.4% net margin** on the buyer-facing total.

That looks healthy because the labor isn't reflected in the cost basis. At 20 minutes per pair (10 minutes assembly, 10 minutes admin/photo/listing pro-rated), the hourly rate works out to $29.37 — solid for handmade jewelry.

## The common mistake: pricing brass like sterling

The most expensive mistake in earring economics is failing to segment by material tier. Buyers searching "brass earrings" expect $14–22 retail. Buyers searching "sterling silver earrings" expect $28–48. Buyers searching "14k gold-fill earrings" expect $42–68. Listing a $22 sterling silver earring undercuts the entire premium segment — and on the cost side, you're operating at break-even after labor.

The other failure: anti-tarnish handling. Sellers who skip the rhodium plating or anti-tarnish step lose 20–30% of long-term reviews to "they turned my ears green" complaints. The cost is $0.50 per pair; the reputation hit is permanent.

## How to fix it

- **Segment listings by material in titles explicitly.** "Sterling silver" or "14k gold-fill" in the title commands the price premium. "Earrings" alone does not.
- **Stud + dangle bundles.** A $22 stud + $32 matching dangle pair set sells at $48 and is the highest-converting earring SKU. Buyers want the matched set.
- **Birthstone variants.** Twelve identical earrings with different stones is twelve listings with no additional design work. SEO captures the birthstone-specific long-tail.
- **Opt out of Off-Site Ads under $10k.** Earring buyers convert through Instagram, not Google Shopping. Etsy attribution at 15% is mostly dead weight.
- **Photograph on a model's ear, not on flat lay alone.** This is the single highest-leverage listing change for jewelry. Modeled photos convert 2–3× higher.
