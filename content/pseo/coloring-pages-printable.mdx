## Printable coloring pages: where flat fees eat 13%

Activity printables — coloring pages, mazes, learning sheets — are the canonical low-price digital category on Etsy. Most listings sit at $3–8, and the math at those price points is genuinely brutal. The flat $0.20 listing fee plus the $0.25 payment processing flat fee is $0.45, which is 9% of a $5 sale before any percentage-based fee touches the order. There's a structural reason successful printable shops all eventually pivot to bundles.

## A $5 coloring page sale, fully decomposed

$5 page, no shipping, no cost of goods. Etsy fees:

- Listing fee: $0.20
- Transaction fee (6.5% of $5): $0.33
- Payment processing (3% + $0.25): $0.40
- Off-Site Ads at 15%: $0.75

Baseline: **$0.93 — 18.6% of revenue.** With Off-Site Ads: **$1.68 — 33.6% of revenue.** Net profit: **$4.07 baseline, $3.32 with Off-Site Ads.**

A 33.6% effective fee rate is among the worst on Etsy. That happens because flat fees ($0.45) consume 9% of revenue _before_ any percentage fee — a fixed tax that gets worse as price drops. At $3, the flat-fee tax alone is 15%. At $10, it's 4.5%.

## The common mistake: per-page listings

Most coloring page shops list each design individually at $3–6. The volume needed to make a living is staggering: at $3.32 net per sale on an Off-Site Ads attribution, you need 300 sales/month to clear $1,000. That's 300 separate orders, 300 flat-fee hits, 300 attribution risks.

The shops that work in this category sell _packs_. A 50-page activity bundle at $15 has the same flat fees ($0.45) but 3x the revenue absorption. Effective rate drops from 18.6% to about 9% baseline.

## How to fix it

- **Lead with bundles, not pages.** 20-page, 50-page, and 100-page packs at $9, $15, and $24 are the right unit structure. Singles become upsells, not the main product.
- **Niche down to a topic.** "Coloring pages" is too generic; "Dinosaur coloring pages for ages 4–8" is searchable and converts.
- **Activity packs > single-format packs.** Mixing coloring pages, mazes, and matching games into one $18 bundle outperforms a $15 100-page coloring-only bundle. Parents buy variety.
- **Opt out of Off-Site Ads under $10k.** The 15% drag at $5 price points is catastrophic. Coloring pages spread on Pinterest organically — the paid attribution rarely justifies itself.
- **Subscription play off-Etsy.** A $5/month "new pack every month" subscription on Gumroad or Patreon captures repeat buyers Etsy never can. Etsy charges you fees on every recurring transaction; subscription platforms don't.
